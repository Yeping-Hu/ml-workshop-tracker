# Contributor terms

These terms keep the project's legal footing simple and its data open, for
contributors and maintainers alike. By submitting a contribution — a pull
request, or workshop information via the issue form — you agree to them.

## 1. Licensing in = licensing out

- **Code** contributions are licensed under this repository's [MIT License](LICENSE).
- **Data** contributions (everything under `data/` and workshop details submitted
  through the issue form) are licensed under
  [CC-BY-4.0](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/).

You keep the copyright to your contribution; you grant the project (and everyone
else, via those licenses) the right to use it. There is no copyright assignment
and no separate CLA to sign. This mirrors the default inbound=outbound norm of
open-source collaboration (see also GitHub's Terms of Service, section D.6).

## 2. Certification of origin

By contributing, you certify the
[Developer Certificate of Origin v1.1](https://developercertificate.org/):
in short, that you wrote the contribution or otherwise have the right to submit
it under the licenses above, and that you understand the contribution is public
and a record of it is retained indefinitely.

## 3. The data stays open

Workshop data in this repository is and will remain publicly available under
CC-BY-4.0. Re-licensing community-contributed data under more restrictive terms,
or putting it behind a paywall, is off the table.

## 4. Sustainability

The maintainer may accept donations or sponsorship, and may offer paid services
*around* the project (e.g. hosted alerts, support, or commercial data exports of
the same openly licensed data) to sustain it. None of that changes points 1–3.

## 5. Conduct

Be kind, assume good faith, and keep workshop entries factual with links to
official sources. Entries that can't be verified may be removed.
